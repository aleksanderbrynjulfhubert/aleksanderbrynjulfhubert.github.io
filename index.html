Aleksander Brynjulf Hübert

I'm Aleksander a recent MSc student in Applicable Mathematics at the London School of Economics and Political Science where my Master's Dissertation was entitled
Turing Completeness, Logic Gates, and the Universality of Conway’s Game of Life.

Before studying at the LSE I obtained my Bachelor's of Science in Quantitative Sciences Linguistics Track and Mathematics, at Emory University in Atlanta. While at Emory, I worked as a Digital Scholarship Intern for the Emory Center for Digital Scholarship and a Teacher's Assistant for Statistical Computing I/II for Emory's Institute for Quantitative Theory and Methods.

My interest in maps and the way we communicate ideas about the world around us lead me to work in the geospatial realm. Previously working as a GIS Intern and Surveyor for the American Excavations of Samothrace and as a Cartographer and Graphic Designer for Anteres Norge AS.

Along with my acadmeic and work interests I have passions in film, linguistics, music, and design. I enjoy working with maps and visualizations and like to be able explore language and the world through sound and visuals.
