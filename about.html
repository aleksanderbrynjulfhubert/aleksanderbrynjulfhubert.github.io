about me

I'm Aleksander, a Norwegian-American MSc student in Applicable Mathematics at the London School of Economics and Political Science.

I obtained my Bachelor's of Science in Quantitative Sciences, Math, and Linguistics at Emory University in Atlanta. While at Emory, I worked as a Digital Scholarship Intern for the Emory Center for Digital Scholarship and a Teacher's Assistant for Statistical Computing I/II for Emory's Institute for Quantitative Theory and Methods.

I also worked as a GIS Intern and Surveyor for the American Excavations of Samothrace and as a Cartographer and Graphic Designer for Anteres Norge AS.

Along with my acadmeics I have interests in film, linguistics, music, and design. I enjoy working with maps and visualizations and like to be able explore the world through sound and visuals.
